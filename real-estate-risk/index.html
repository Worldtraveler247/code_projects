What does a score of 55 mean?

A composite score of 55 is "Moderate" — indicators are on average about 0.25 standard deviations above their 20-year norm. The market is running a touch hot but is not in obvious bubble territory. Normal late-cycle behavior; not a buy signal, not a sell signal. Watch the trajectory: a score that\'s been climbing 5+ points per quarter is more concerning than a steady 55.

When to consider buying — by asset class

Heuristic guidance only — not investment advice. Real estate buying decisions depend on local conditions, your timeline, financing, and personal circumstances no national index can capture.

Asset ClassBest entries historicallyWhy
Single-Family Homes Score 75+ AND falling for 2+ quarters Residential bottoms appear 12–18 months after the peak in distress signals. 2009–2012 and (locally) 2022–2024 fit this pattern.
Commercial Real Estate Score 70+, with delinquency >5% and rates falling CRE bottoms after residential by 6–12 months. The 2010–2013 CRE bottom lagged residential by ~18 months. Wait for distressed sales to appear.
Hotels & Hospitality Score 80+, with unemployment peaking and travel demand troughed The most cyclical real estate. Hotels trade at fire-sale prices when occupancy collapses (2009, 2020). Recovery lags broader economy by 12–24 months but the upside is the largest of the three.

Score-based timing alone is a blunt tool. The professional approach combines this risk score with: cap rates vs. Treasury yields (CRE), price-to-rent (residential), and RevPAR trends (hotels). The score tells you when the macro is favorable; specific deals still require local underwriting.

What each level signals

  • 0–25 Minimal: Indicators below their long-run averages. Healthy expansion territory; rare.
  • 25–45 Low: Below average risk. Reasonable late-recovery / mid-cycle reading.
  • 45–65 Moderate: Around long-run average. Elevated only if trajectory is rising.
  • 65–80 High: Multiple indicators in their top quintile. Watch closely; bubble or late-cycle.
  • 80–100 Critical: Multiple indicators in their top decile or showing 2σ extremes. Either a crash is underway or one is forming. 2007 reached this level; 2008–2010 sat there.

Methodology & Caveats

Each indicator is scored on a 0-100 scale based on its z-score against the previous 20 years. A score of 50 represents the historical average; values above 65 enter "high risk" territory; above 80 = "critical." The composite is the unweighted mean of all available indicators. Direction is set per indicator — for some, higher = riskier (Case-Shiller, delinquencies), for others, lower = riskier (yield curve, sentiment).

This is not investment advice and not a prediction. Real estate crashes are rare, multi-causal, and notoriously hard to predict in real time. This dashboard surfaces statistical anomalies in indicators that have historically preceded major housing corrections (1989 S&L crisis, 2008 GFC). Use it as one input among many.
