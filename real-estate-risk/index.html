Methodology & Caveats

Each indicator is scored on a 0-100 scale based on its z-score against the previous 20 years. A score of 50 represents the historical average; values above 65 enter "high risk" territory; above 80 = "critical." The composite is the unweighted mean of all available indicators. Direction is set per indicator — for some, higher = riskier (Case-Shiller, delinquencies), for others, lower = riskier (yield curve, sentiment).

This is not investment advice and not a prediction. Real estate crashes are rare, multi-causal, and notoriously hard to predict in real time. This dashboard surfaces statistical anomalies in indicators that have historically preceded major housing corrections (1989 S&L crisis, 2008 GFC). Use it as one input among many.

Data: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) · St. Louis Fed · refreshed daily via GitHub Actions
